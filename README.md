# Tata GenAI Powered Data Analytics Virtual Experience — 

# Project Overview

This project focused on using AI and predictive analytics in financial services to identify customers who are at risk of missing payments and becoming delinquent. The project also explored how businesses can use AI responsibly to improve collections strategies, automate customer outreach, and maintain fairness and compliance.

The project simulated the role of an AI Transformation Consultant working with Geldium, a financial services company facing increasing credit card delinquency rates.

The overall goal was to:
- Analyze customer financial data
- Identify delinquency risk factors
- Design predictive models
- Create business recommendations
- Build a responsible AI-powered collections strategy

# TASK 1 — Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

# What happened?

In Task 1, the main objective was to understand the dataset before building any predictive model. This involved checking data quality, identifying missing values, detecting anomalies, and understanding which customer behaviors indicate delinquency risk.

The dataset contained financial and behavioral customer information such as:
- Income
- Credit Score
- Credit Utilization
- Missed Payments
- Debt-to-Income Ratio
- Payment History
- Employment Status

The goal was to understand how these variables influence customer delinquency.

--------------------------------------------------

# Important Concepts Learned

## EDA (Exploratory Data Analysis)
Understanding and exploring data before modeling.

## Missing Values
Empty or unavailable data entries.

## Outliers
Unusual or abnormal values in the dataset.

## Imputation
Filling missing data using statistical techniques.

## Correlation
Relationship between variables.

## Risk Indicators
Variables showing higher probability of delinquency.

## Synthetic Data
Artificially generated realistic data used when real data is incomplete.

--------------------------------------------------

# Main Business Understanding

This task showed that AI systems are only reliable when the underlying data is clean, complete, representative, and unbiased.

Poor data quality can lead to:
- inaccurate predictions,
- unfair outcomes,
- weak business decisions.

--------------------------------------------------

# TASK 2 — Predictive Modeling

# What happened?

In Task 2, the goal was to design a predictive model capable of identifying customers likely to become delinquent.

Different AI and machine learning models were explored, including:
- Logistic Regression
- Decision Trees
- Neural Networks

The task focused on selecting the best model based on:
- explainability,
- fairness,
- business suitability,
- and operational practicality.

--------------------------------------------------

# Important Concepts Learned

## Predictive Modeling
Using historical data to predict future outcomes.

## Logistic Regression
A model used for yes/no predictions such as delinquent vs non-delinquent.

## Decision Tree
A branching model that makes decisions step-by-step using conditions.

## Neural Network
An advanced AI model capable of learning complex patterns.

## Accuracy
Measures overall correctness of predictions.

## Precision
Measures how many predicted risky customers were actually risky.

## Recall
Measures how many actual risky customers were successfully identified.

## F1 Score
Balance between precision and recall.

## Explainability
Ability to explain why AI made a prediction.

## Bias
Unfair treatment of certain customer groups by AI systems.

## Fairness
Ensuring equal and responsible treatment across customer segments.

--------------------------------------------------

# Main Business Understanding

This task demonstrated that financial institutions often prefer explainable and transparent AI systems over highly complex black-box models because financial decisions require accountability, trust, and regulatory compliance.

Logistic Regression was selected because it provides:
- transparency,
- probability-based outputs,
- explainability,
- and operational simplicity.

# Important Concepts Learned

## Agentic AI
AI systems capable of autonomous reasoning, decision-making, and adaptation.

## Human-in-the-loop
Humans supervising important AI decisions.

## Learning Loop
Continuous improvement of AI systems using new outcomes and feedback.

## Data Pipeline

## Decision Engine
System component responsible for making AI-driven decisions.

## Action Layer
Part of the system that executes interventions such as reminders or outreach.

## Compliance

AI systems that are fair, transparent, explainable, and ethically governed.
